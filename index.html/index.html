Biography

I was born in Addis Ababa in 2005 G.C. and raised in a family that faced significant financial hardship. My early childhood was shaped by instability and sacrifice. I spent my first four years living with my father, Kassahun Abebayehu, before my mother, Roza Alemu, migrated to Dubai in search of better opportunities to support our family.

After her departure, I lived with my aunt for a short period until her untimely death. Soon after, my parents divorced, marking another major turning point in my life. I later reunited with my mother and experienced a more stable living standard, which allowed me to focus on my education and personal growth.

Tragically, when I was twelve years old, my mother passed away. I lived alone for three years, a period that forced me to mature far earlier than most. During this time, I survived and progressed with the unwavering support of my uncle, Tilahun Alemu, whose guidance and care profoundly shaped the person I am today. Out of deep respect and love, I proudly carry his name.

These experiences taught me resilience, discipline, and purpose. They transformed hardship into motivation and loss into ambition. Today, I pursue Economics with a clear vision: to rise beyond my circumstances and create lasting impact through knowledge, analysis, and leadership.
